## Design Context

### Users

Internal administrators and sales professionals working in a shared business operations dashboard. Admins need trustworthy analytics, team oversight, permissions, and global CRM visibility. Sales users need a fast, action-oriented workspace for assigned leads, contact activity, and follow-ups. The product should support dense daily work on desktop while preserving complete, efficient workflows on mobile.

### Brand Personality

Precise, composed, and quietly premium. The interface should inspire confidence and clarity, feel mature enough for consequential business decisions, and remain warm enough for sustained daily use.

### Aesthetic Direction

A refined, editorial enterprise interface with the restraint and craft associated with products such as Linear and Vercel. Use crisp typography, softly tinted neutral surfaces, a controlled accent palette, intentional asymmetry, and strong information hierarchy. Support fully considered light and dark themes. Avoid decorative gradients, neon color, glassmorphism, cartoonish illustration, oversized headings, random card colors, excessive rounding, and generic dashboard templates.

### Design Principles

1. Make the next useful action obvious; prioritize operational clarity over vanity metrics.
2. Use color semantically and sparingly, with perceptually balanced neutrals and accessible contrast.
3. Create hierarchy through typography, spacing, alignment, and density before adding containers or decoration.
4. Adapt workflows for mobile rather than shrinking or hiding essential desktop functionality.
5. Treat every state as designed: loading, empty, error, focus, hover, selected, and permission-restricted.
6. Build reusable primitives and module configuration so future dashboard areas inherit the same quality without duplication.
